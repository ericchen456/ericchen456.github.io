projects

Personal Projects:

Time Finder:

In track, athletes are typically given a heat sheet that tells them who they are competing against. Colin Jennings and I worked together to create a program where you can copy and paste your heat sheet and it will return each athlete's name as well as their personal records in the given event. Currently in progress.

Variant Chess Engine:

Chess engines are computers that evaluate positions and determine the best possible move(s) in each position. They are powerful enough to easily beat the best human chess players. Chess variants are some sort of slight variation on the original rules of chess. However, a large majority of chess variants do not have a computer engine. I worked together with Sahith Edula to create an elementary level computer engine for popular chess variants such as duck chess, crazyhouse, and three-check. Currently in progress. Currently capable of playing regular chess, no illegal moves.

Introduction to Engineering Design:

3D Illusion Lamp:

We were tasked with creating a design that would be cut into acryllic in such a way that, when LED lights in the bottom of the lamp base turned on, it would light up the design. It taught me how the basics of CAD Modeling and served as great practice for using a dial caliper.

Automata Box:

We were tasked with creating an animation or story by utilizing two or three cams to simulate motion. It taught me how to laser cut, assemble parts, sand parts down, and introduced me to the basics of utilizing mechanical motion to create the intended outcome.
